Vertical · Healthcare
Patient-Facing & Intake Staff
Intake coordinators, patient access specialists, front desk — the first person a patient reaches. High stakes, high emotion, regulatory exposure on every call.
HC · 01
No Available Appointments
Patient calls in distress needing to be seen today. The schedule has no openings. Patient says they can't wait and begins to escalate.
Care access · Patient safety
HC · 02
Out-of-Network Denial Mid-Treatment
Patient received a denial notice on a claim for care already received. They're asking the intake worker to explain why — and to fix it. The worker handles scheduling, not billing.
Financial distress · Scope boundary
HC · 03
Patient Refusing to Provide Information
Intake requires date of birth, insurance ID, and reason for visit. The patient declines to answer some questions, citing privacy. Without the information, intake can't proceed.
HIPAA compliance · Workflow block
HC · 04
Medical Records Request with Legal Questions
A caller requests records for a custody or legal proceeding. They ask what can be released, when, and to whom. The worker knows the record release process — but not the legal answer.
HIPAA · Legal liability · Scope

Vertical · Financial Services
Branch Bankers & Loan Officers
Universal bankers, personal bankers, loan officers — frontline advisors navigating regulatory constraints, compliance floors, and customers who arrived already upset.
FS · 01
Loan Denial — Customer Wants to Dispute It Now
A customer sits across from the banker and learns their application was denied. They ask why, want it reversed, and want to speak to whoever made the call. The banker didn't make it.
Regulatory · Adverse action · Emotion
FS · 02
Overdraft Fee Dispute — Demands a Manager
Customer arrives or calls demanding a fee reversal. They've been a customer for years. The banker can reverse one but not three. The customer threatens to close the account if all three aren't waived.
Retention · Policy limit · Escalation
FS · 03
Identity Documents Don't Match
Customer presents ID that doesn't match what's on the account. They have an explanation. The banker needs to verify — but the policy is clear, and verifying requires a process the customer finds offensive.
Fraud risk · Compliance · Dignity
FS · 04
Customer Asking for Financial Advice
Customer asks where they should put their money, whether they should pay off their mortgage early, or how to invest their inheritance. The banker can offer products. Not advice. The line is thin and live.
Regulatory · Liability · Relationship
→
Discovery question
"Which of these creates the most inconsistency across your branch staff? Is there a right answer your team doesn't have documented anywhere?"
Vertical · Government Benefits
Benefits Specialists & Claims Examiners
Unemployment, housing, SNAP, workforce development staff — workers navigating the intersection of eligibility rules, desperate claimants, and systems that don't explain themselves.
GB · 01
Denial — Claimant Demands Immediate Reversal
Claimant receives a denial determination and calls in. They need the benefits to pay rent this week. They believe the decision is wrong and want it fixed on this call. The worker can't reverse it on this call.
Housing security · Process constraint
GB · 02
Missed Appeal Window
Claimant calls 35 days after a denial. The appeal window closed at 30 days. They didn't know. They have a reason. The worker must explain that the window has passed — and what, if anything, comes next.
Finality · Distress · Limited options
GB · 03
Overpayment Notice — Claimant Can't Repay
Claimant receives a notice that they were overpaid and must repay $2,100. They spent it. They have no way to pay it back. They want someone to tell them this can go away. The worker can't make that call.
Financial hardship · Waiver process
GB · 04
Language Barrier During Eligibility Determination
Caller's primary language is not English. A key eligibility question requires nuanced clarification. An interpreter is theoretically available — but getting one connected takes time the caller is not willing to wait for.
Access · Equity · Compliance

Vertical · Insurance
Claims Adjusters & Intake Representatives
First notice of loss staff, intake reps, desk adjusters — the first contact when something has gone wrong in a policyholder's life. Stakes are high. The caller already knows it.
IN · 01
First Notice of Loss — Emotionally Distressed Claimant
Claimant calls immediately after a house fire, a car accident, or a storm event. They are not ready for a structured intake process. They need to be heard before anything else can happen.
De-escalation · Intake accuracy · Trust
IN · 02
Claimant Disputing a Settlement Offer
Adjuster communicates a settlement amount. Claimant says it's not enough to cover their actual loss. They've gotten their own estimate. The adjuster doesn't set the number — and can't change it on this call.
Scope · Litigation risk · Relationship
IN · 03
CAT Event — High-Volume Surge Call
Major weather event hit last night. The queue is 200 calls deep. Each caller believes their situation is the most urgent. The adjuster has 8 minutes per call. The caller has been without power for 18 hours.
Volume · Triage · Consistency
IN · 04
Coverage Question the Adjuster Can't Answer
Claimant asks whether a specific loss type is covered under their policy. The adjuster isn't sure. Saying yes creates a liability. Saying no might be wrong. Saying "I don't know" damages trust.
Accuracy · Liability · Confidence
→
Discovery question
"Which of these does your team face most often? And which one do they handle most differently depending on the adjuster?"
Vertical · Retail & Hospitality
Floor Associates & Front Desk Staff
Store associates, front desk staff, floor supervisors — workers who manage live customer situations in real time, in public, with no escalation room and a line behind them.
RH · 01
Refund Request Outside the Policy Window
Customer has a receipt. The item is unused. But they're 4 days past the return window. They want the refund. The associate can see the policy clearly. The customer believes it shouldn't apply to them.
Policy enforcement · Retention · Pressure
RH · 02
Out-of-Stock Escalation
Customer drove to the store for a specific item that's listed as in stock online. It isn't. The associate didn't create the problem. The customer doesn't care. They want the item or something equivalent.
Expectation gap · Live resolution
RH · 03
Verbally Aggressive Customer — Other Customers Present
A customer is raising their voice at an associate. Other customers are watching. The situation is escalating. The associate needs to de-escalate without appearing to give in — and without creating a scene.
Safety · Brand · Witness pressure
RH · 04
Complaint About Another Employee
Customer tells the associate they were treated badly by a colleague. They want the associate to validate their experience and take action. The associate doesn't have that authority — and the colleague is still on the floor.
HR boundary · Customer trust · Scope
→
Discovery question

Vertical · Utilities
Customer Service Representatives
Utility CSRs navigating billing disputes, service outages, and disconnection threats — often as the only point of contact for customers in genuine hardship.
UT · 01
Past-Due Account — Disconnection Threatened
Customer's account is 60 days past due. Disconnection is scheduled for tomorrow. Customer says they can pay half now. The rep needs to determine whether that stops the disconnection — and communicate it clearly without making a promise they can't keep.
Housing safety · Policy limit · Trust
UT · 02
Bill Dispute — Customer Believes It's Wrong
Customer's bill is three times higher than normal. They want an explanation and a correction on this call. The rep can see the usage data — but not why usage spiked. The customer believes the meter is wrong.
Accuracy · Trust · Investigation lag
UT · 03
Outage Report — Customer Claims It's Targeted
Customer reports an outage and says their neighbors have power. They believe they were shut off punitively for a billing dispute. The rep sees a real outage. The customer doesn't believe them.
Trust · Verification · Escalation
UT · 04
Non-English Speaker During Service Call
Customer calls to report a gas smell or a service issue. Their English is limited. The rep is not bilingual. The issue may be a safety matter. Every second the call takes longer is a second the situation could worsen.
Safety · Access · Equity
