Patient access · Billing · Member services

Healthcare Contact Center

Gate: No clinical advice — HIPAA
S-01 · Healthcare
Insurance Eligibility Failure
Patient calls to schedule or after an appointment. Agent runs eligibility — coverage shows inactive, wrong plan, or out-of-network. Patient insists they have insurance.
Where it breaks
Agent can’t confirm coverage without payer portal access; can’t tell patient what IS covered; can’t promise the service will be paid. Must navigate without creating liability.
“Does this situation come up for your agents?”
S-02 · Healthcare
Billing Dispute — Post-Procedure
Patient received a bill after a procedure and is disputing the amount, the line items, or claiming insurance should have covered it. Caller is often distressed — the bill followed bad news.
Where it breaks
Agent sees the bill but can’t modify it, promise a refund, or make a coverage determination. Must acknowledge the frustration without overpromising.
“Does this situation come up for your agents?”
S-03 · Healthcare
Prior Authorization Hold
Patient needs a procedure or medication. Physician ordered it. Insurance requires prior auth. Patient calls asking why they can’t get their medication or their appointment yet.
Where it breaks
Agent cannot push the payer, cannot expedite the clinical review, and cannot tell the patient the outcome. Must explain the delay without giving clinical advice or false hope.
“Does this situation come up for your agents?”
S-04 · Healthcare
Clinical Escalation — Symptom Triage
Caller describes symptoms or asks whether their condition is serious enough to come in. Agent is not a clinician and cannot make that determination.
Where it breaks
Hard stop. Agent cannot give any clinical guidance. Must transfer to clinical staff without making any medical judgment. A delayed transfer can cause real harm.

Banking agents · Credit union reps · Loan servicing

Financial Services Contact Center

Gate: Identity verified before any account access
S-01 · Financial Services
Suspected Account Takeover
Customer calls saying they didn’t make recent transactions. Or a caller is trying to reset credentials and something about the call feels scripted or off.
Where it breaks
Agent must verify identity before accessing the account — but the fraud may have already compromised it. Cannot take any irreversible action (wire transfer, address change) until fully verified.
“Does this situation come up for your agents?”
S-02 · Financial Services
Transaction Dispute — Unauthorized Charge
Customer says a charge on their account is unauthorized. Wants it reversed immediately. May be escalating.
Where it breaks
Agent cannot promise a reversal timeline or guarantee the outcome of a dispute investigation. Must open the dispute correctly in the system without creating false expectations.
“Does this situation come up for your agents?”
S-03 · Financial Services
Account Access Locked
Customer can’t log in, has been locked out, or can’t pass knowledge-based authentication. Escalating on the phone. Wants the agent to “just let them in.”
Where it breaks
Agent cannot bypass security protocols. Must follow the exact unlock process. If the caller is a fraudster, any shortcut is a completed security breach.
“Does this situation come up for your agents?”
S-04 · Financial Services
Loan Payoff — Exact Balance Inquiry
Customer needs the exact payoff amount for a mortgage or auto loan — often for a closing or refinance. Wants the precise number right now on the phone.
Where it breaks
Verbal payoff quotes may not be legally binding. Figures change daily with interest accrual. Agent cannot give a final number the customer can rely on for a legal closing.
“Does this situation come up for your agents?”

Benefits navigators · Eligibility specialists · State agency reps

Government Benefits Contact Center

Gate: Cannot determine eligibility or promise benefits
S-01 · Government Benefits
Eligibility Question — Do I Qualify?
Caller wants to know if they qualify for SNAP, Medicaid, or unemployment. They describe their situation and ask directly: “Do I qualify?”
Where it breaks
Agent cannot make the eligibility determination — that’s the system’s job. Must intake the information and direct to the application process without answering the question directly.
“Does this situation come up for your agents?”
S-02 · Government Benefits
Application Status — Where Is My Case?
Caller submitted an application days or weeks ago and wants to know what’s happening. They may have a deadline — food is running out, rent is due.
Where it breaks
Agent may not have visibility into the processing queue or reviewer decisions. Cannot promise a timeline or expedite without proper authority. Stakes are high.
“Does this situation come up for your agents?”
S-03 · Government Benefits
Document Gap — I Submitted That Already
Caller says they submitted required documents. System shows documents missing or not received. Caller is frustrated and may be near a redetermination deadline.
Where it breaks
Agent cannot verify what the caller submitted on their end. Cannot manually mark documents as received. Must navigate without invalidating the caller’s claim or dismissing their frustration.
“Does this situation come up for your agents?”
S-04 · Government Benefits
Benefit Denial — Why Was I Cut Off?
Caller received a denial letter or notice that their benefit is being reduced or terminated. They want to know why and what they can do about it.
Where it breaks
Agent must explain the determination without overturning it, and must provide the correct appeals path without giving legal advice. Caller’s income may depend on this call.
“Does this situation come up for your agents?”

Customer service agents · Outage specialists · Billing reps

Utilities Contact Center

Gate: Cannot promise restoration times or field dates
S-01 · Utilities
Outage During High-Volume Event
Major weather event or equipment failure. Agent is handling 10x normal call volume. Caller demands to know when power, water, or gas will be restored.
Where it breaks
Agent has no direct line to field restoration crews. Restoration estimates come from the OMS — not the agent. Promising a time that doesn’t hold makes the situation worse.
“Does this situation come up for your agents?”
S-02 · Utilities
High Bill Dispute — Alleged Meter Error
Customer received an unusually high bill and believes the meter is wrong, the rate changed without notice, or usage was misread. Wants answers now.
Where it breaks
Agent cannot confirm a meter error without a field investigation. Cannot reverse the bill on the spot. Must take the complaint and set expectations for the investigation process.
“Does this situation come up for your agents?”
S-03 · Utilities
Payment Arrangement — Behind on Bill
Customer is behind on their bill and asking for a payment plan to avoid shutoff. May be in crisis.
Where it breaks
Agent cannot set up unauthorized payment plans or promise shutoff protection without system authority. Mathematical errors in plans create liability. Inconsistency across agents creates compliance exposure.
“Does this situation come up for your agents?”
S-04 · Utilities
Service Setup or Transfer — Moving
Customer is moving. Needs to set up service at a new address or transfer from an existing one. May need a field technician visit. Has a move-in date.
Where it breaks
Agent must coordinate new account setup and cannot commit to a service activation date without field availability. “We’ll be there Thursday” without confirmation creates broken promises.
“Does this situation come up for your agents?”

Claims intake agents · Policy servicing reps · Member services

Insurance Contact Center

Gate: Agent is recorder — not adjudicator
S-01 · Insurance
First Notice of Loss (FNOL) Intake
Policyholder just had an incident — car accident, house fire, storm damage. First call to report the claim. Caller may be distressed or in shock.
Where it breaks
Agent must accurately document the incident without making any statement about coverage or liability. “Sounds like that would be covered” creates insurer liability. The agent is the recorder, not the adjudicator.
“Does this situation come up for your agents?”
S-02 · Insurance
Claim Status — Why Is This Taking So Long?
Policyholder filed a claim weeks ago. Calling to find out where it is. The adjuster hasn’t been in touch. They need an answer today.
Where it breaks
Agent may not have adjuster contact or visibility into the claim queue. Cannot force the adjuster to call. Cannot estimate when the claim will be resolved. High frustration potential.
“Does this situation come up for your agents?”
S-03 · Insurance
Coverage Dispute — I Thought This Was Covered
Policyholder expected their policy to cover a procedure, repair, or loss. They’re calling because a claim was denied or a bill was sent directly to them.
Where it breaks
Agent can explain what the policy says, but cannot override the denial or make a new coverage determination. Must explain without inflaming the situation further.
“Does this situation come up for your agents?”
S-04 · Insurance
Lapse Risk — Missed Payment
Policyholder missed a payment. Policy is at risk of lapsing — or may have already lapsed. Caller wants to know if they’re still covered. May have an active incident.
Where it breaks
Agent cannot confirm coverage for an in-progress incident if the policy may have lapsed. Must follow reinstatement process without creating ambiguity about current coverage status.
“Does this situation come up for your agents?”

Customer service agents · Order support reps · Returns specialists

Retail Contact Center

Gate: Cannot override policy or control carrier
S-01 · Retail
Order Not Received — Carrier Shows Delivered
Customer says they never received their package. Carrier tracking says “delivered.” Customer wants a refund or replacement right now.
Where it breaks
Agent cannot override what the carrier says. Cannot promise reshipment or refund without completing the claims process. “Delivered” status creates a dispute that takes time — but the customer wants it resolved now.
“Does this situation come up for your agents?”
S-02 · Retail
Return Outside the Policy Window
Customer wants to return an item but the return window has closed. They have a reason — gift, medical, travel. They want an exception.
Where it breaks
Agent cannot make policy exceptions without authorization. Verbal exceptions create inconsistency across the team and can’t be systematically honored. Must hold policy without destroying the relationship.
“Does this situation come up for your agents?”
S-03 · Retail
Wrong Item / Damaged Product
Customer received the wrong product or something that arrived damaged. Wants it fixed immediately. Doesn’t want to wait for a process.
Where it breaks
Agent must initiate the replacement or refund process correctly — including whether a photo is required, whether the original ships back, and what timeline is realistic. Cannot overpromise.
“Does this situation come up for your agents?”
S-04 · Retail
Supervisor Demand — Escalation Request
Customer is frustrated about any of the above and demands to speak with a supervisor right now. They don’t want to hear from the agent anymore.
Where it breaks
Agent must de-escalate or transfer — but cannot tell the customer a supervisor will “definitely fix this” or override the prior answer. Escalation must be managed without invalidating the original response.
“Does this situation come up for your agents?”
