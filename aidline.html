Process

How a guide gets built.

Done-for-you. You send the source material. We deliver a finished, deployable HTML guide. Your agents open it in any browser — no software required.

01 — Scope

Discovery Call

We research your operation before we get on the call. You describe your most common situations. We scope the guide. You receive a written proposal.

02 — Source

You Send the Material

SOPs, job aids, knowledge base articles, call recordings — whatever you have. We work with what exists. We do not need it organized.

03 — Build

We Structure It

Every situation gets case setup, a step sequence, an agent script, and a case note template — built inside the Five-Operation Panel Architecture.

04 — Deliver

You Receive the Guide

A single HTML file. Opens in any browser. No internet required after delivery. Share it, pin it, deploy it. Your guide, your branding.

The Work Behind the Work

Built from inside
the problem.

Aidline was not designed in a conference room. It was built by someone who sat in the agent seat — handling federal employee FSA claims, navigating state unemployment systems, and working inside contact center environments where the calls came in faster than the training covered them.

The gap was always the same: multiple systems, constantly shifting policies, high-stakes callers, and no single reference that met the agent where the call was actually happening.

Rudolph CX exists because that guide should have existed — and now it does. Built to your operation. Structured to your situations. Delivered as a finished tool your agents can open the moment the call comes in.
