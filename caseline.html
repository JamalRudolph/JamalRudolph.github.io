Does this moment require a mandatory report?

  • Did the client disclose abuse, neglect, or exploitation of a child, elder, or dependent adult?
  • Is there credible risk of imminent harm to self or others?
  • Did you observe indicators of human trafficking? (TVPA)
  • Did the client disclose information that triggers your jurisdiction's mandated reporter statute?
  • Does CAPTA apply to this population and this disclosure?
  • Is Elder Justice Act APS reporting triggered? (Final Rule, June 7, 2024)
⚑ IF ANY BOX APPLIES → REPORT OBLIGATION IS NOT DISCRETIONARY. Proceed to your agency's mandatory reporting protocol before continuing the session. Documentation must reflect the disclosure and your action.

01 Before every client session Always First ⌄
  1. 1.Run the Mandatory Reporting check above. Fully. Every time.
  2. 2.Confirm client identity and current supervision status (if applicable to population).
  3. 3.Review any open action items from prior session before adding new ones.
  4. 4.Note any disclosures that may have changed the client's legal overlay (new arrest, custody change, benefit termination).
Hard Stop
You are not required to complete a session before filing a mandatory report. The report obligation fires on disclosure — not on session completion.

S-01Mandatory Reporting Trigger Hard Stop⌄
  1. 1.Stop the session immediately upon disclosure or observation that triggers your mandated reporter obligation.
  2. 2.Identify which statute applies: CAPTA (child), Elder Justice Act APS Final Rule June 7 2024 (elder/adult), TVPA (trafficking).
  3. 3.File report per your jurisdiction's protocol within the required timeframe. Do not defer to end of session.
  4. 4.Document the disclosure verbatim in the case record. Do not paraphrase client language.
  5. 5.Notify supervisor per agency policy. Mandatory reporting is not a supervisory decision — it is your individual obligation.
CAPTA / Elder Justice Act
Mandatory reporting is not discretionary. Client consent is not required. Confidentiality does not override the report obligation.
Source: CAPTA 42 U.S.C. § 5101 · Elder Justice Act APS Final Rule (June 7, 2024; compliance date May 8, 2028) · TVPA (FBI documented indicators)
S-02HIPAA Disclosure Decision HIPAA⌄
  1. 1.Identify who is requesting the disclosure and under what authority.
  2. 2.Confirm whether a signed authorization exists. If yes — verify it is current, complete, and covers this specific disclosure.
  3. 3.Apply Minimum Necessary standard: disclose only what the purpose requires. (45 CFR § 164.514(d))
  4. 4.If subpoena: attorney subpoena alone is insufficient. Court order or client authorization required for PHI. (45 CFR § 164.512(e))
  5. 5.Document the disclosure: date, recipient, purpose, what was shared, authorization basis.
Information Blocking
Withholding records a client has a right to access is a violation of the 21st Century Cures Act (45 CFR Part 171), enforceable since April 2021.
Right of Access Deadline
30-day hard deadline. One 30-day extension with written notice. OCR enforcement active — $112,500 penalty (Concentra, Dec 2025).
Source: HIPAA 45 CFR Parts 160/164 · Right of Access 45 CFR § 164.524 · 21st Century Cures Act 45 CFR Part 171
S-03SNAP / TANF Eligibility Determination Federal Benefits⌄
  1. 1.Confirm household composition and income against current federal poverty level.
  2. 2.For SNAP: check for drug felony conviction. Ban is state-by-state — never assume. Verify your state's current policy.
  3. 3.For TANF: confirm work participation requirements and exemptions applicable to this client.
  4. 4.Document all verification documents collected and submitted.
  5. 5.Inform client of appeal rights and timeline if application is denied or benefits are reduced.
Drug Felony Ban — State Variation
SNAP drug felony restriction is state-by-state under PRWORA 1996. Some states have fully lifted it. Never assume eligibility or ineligibility without checking your state's current policy.
Source: SNAP/TANF/Medicaid federal standards · PRWORA 1996
S-04Medicaid Enrollment / Referral Medicaid⌄
  1. 1.Confirm citizenship/immigration status — determines eligibility pathway.
  2. 2.For justice-involved individuals: as of January 1, 2026, Medicaid must be suspended — not terminated — upon incarceration. No new application required upon release. (Consolidated Appropriations Act 2024)
  3. 3.Connect client to enrollment assistance. Do not leave enrollment incomplete at session end.
  4. 4.Document referral and next steps in case record.
Reentry — Medicaid Suspension (Jan 1, 2026)
Justice-involved clients released after Jan 1, 2026 do not need to reapply for Medicaid. Benefits were suspended, not terminated. Bring release papers — reinstatement is the process.
Source: SNAP/TANF/Medicaid · Consolidated Appropriations Act 2024
S-05ADA Accommodation Request ADA Title II⌄
  1. 1.Client does not need to use the word "disability" or cite the ADA to trigger the accommodation obligation.
  2. 2.Engage in the interactive process — ask what the client needs, not whether they qualify.
  3. 3.Document the request, the interactive process, and the accommodation provided or reason for denial.
  4. 4.Do not require medical documentation as a precondition for engaging the interactive process.
WCAG 2.1 AA — Deadline April 24, 2026
ADA Title II now requires WCAG 2.1 AA compliance for digital services used in government-adjacent settings. Non-compliance: $55,000–$150,000 in fines.
Source: ADA Title II · 28 CFR Part 35
S-06Mental Health Crisis — Suicidal Ideation Crisis Protocol⌄
  1. 1.Use the Columbia Suicide Severity Rating Scale (C-SSRS) — the SAMHSA-validated instrument for this population.
  2. 2.Ask directly: "Are you thinking about suicide?" Direct language does not increase risk.
  3. 3.Assess ideation, plan, means, intent, and timeline using C-SSRS framework.
  4. 4.If imminent risk: do not leave client alone. Contact crisis services. Document the assessment and your response.
  5. 5.Warm handoff — do not provide a number and end the session. Stay with the client through the transfer.
SAMHSA TIC — Trauma-Informed Lens
Apply Trauma-Informed Care principles throughout crisis intervention. Power dynamics in the helping relationship affect disclosure. A client who feels controlled will not disclose accurately.
Source: C-SSRS Columbia/SAMHSA · SAMHSA TIC Six Principles (SMA14-4884)
S-07Substance Use Disclosure — 42 CFR Part 2 SUD Confidentiality⌄
  1. 1.SUD treatment records are subject to 42 CFR Part 2 — stricter than HIPAA. General HIPAA authorization is not sufficient.
  2. 2.Disclosure requires a Part 2-specific written consent that names the recipient, the information to be shared, and the purpose.
  3. 3.Confirm whether your program is a Part 2-covered entity before applying these rules.
  4. 4.Document the consent form received and the scope of disclosure authorized.
42 CFR Part 2 — Enforcement Active Feb 16, 2026
Updated enforcement posture is now active. Do not apply pre-2026 Part 2 guidance.
Source: 42 CFR Part 2 SUD records · Enforcement effective February 16, 2026
S-08Housing Instability Referral HUD Housing First⌄
  1. 1.Assess housing status: housed / at-risk / literally homeless / fleeing DV.
  2. 2.Housing First principle: housing access is not contingent on sobriety, compliance, or treatment participation. (HUD CoC NOFO FY2024-2025)
  3. 3.For formerly incarcerated clients: HUD One-Strike proposed rule was withdrawn in 2025. Many formerly incarcerated individuals CAN be eligible for subsidized housing — do not assume ineligibility.
  4. 4.For DV survivors: VAWA protections apply to housing. (24 CFR Part 5 Subpart L)
  5. 5.Document referral, availability, and client's response.
HUD One-Strike — Withdrawn 2025
The proposed rule restricting housing access for formerly incarcerated individuals was withdrawn. Do not apply it. Many formerly incarcerated individuals are eligible for subsidized housing.
Source: HUD Housing First CoC NOFO FY2024-2025 · VAWA 24 CFR Part 5 Subpart L
S-09Elder Abuse / APS Reporting Mandatory Report⌄
  1. 1.Identify indicator type: physical abuse, financial exploitation, neglect, self-neglect, emotional abuse.
  2. 2.Elder Justice Act APS Final Rule (June 7, 2024) — current governing standard. Apply it, not prior guidance.
  3. 3.File APS report per your state's process. Do not delay pending confirmation — report on reasonable suspicion.
  4. 4.OAA Title III services may be applicable for follow-up support.
  5. 5.Document: observed indicators, client statement, your assessment, report filed, date and time.
Elder Justice Act — Final Rule June 7, 2024
This is the governing APS standard. Report on reasonable suspicion — you are not an investigator. Investigation is APS's role, not yours.
Source: Elder Justice Act APS Final Rule (June 7, 2024; compliance date May 8, 2028) · OAA Title III
S-10Trauma-Informed De-escalation High Stakes⌄
  1. 1.Recognize the trigger: agitation, withdrawal, dissociation, or escalating affect may be trauma responses — not defiance.
  2. 2.Lower your voice. Increase space. Reduce environmental stimuli if possible.
  3. 3.Name what you observe without labeling: "I notice you seem frustrated" not "You're being difficult."
  4. 4.Offer the client control: "Would you like to take a break?" "Can we slow down?"
  5. 5.If the situation does not de-escalate: pause the session. Safety of client and worker is the priority.
SAMHSA TIC — Six Principles
Trauma-informed practice is not a technique — it is a lens. Safety, trustworthiness, peer support, collaboration, empowerment, and cultural sensitivity apply throughout every interaction. (SMA14-4884)
Source: SAMHSA TIC Six Principles (SMA14-4884) · NASW Code of Ethics (2021)
S-11Child Welfare / Foster Care ICWA / Title IV-E⌄
  1. 1.At first contact: determine whether the child is or may be an Indian child as defined by ICWA. Ask — do not assume.
  2. 2.If ICWA applies: tribal notification is required. Different placement preferences and standards apply. ICWA governs over state child welfare standards in applicable cases. (25 CFR Part 23)
  3. 3.For foster care placement: confirm Title IV-E eligibility and licensing compliance. (45 CFR ¥ 1356.21)
  4. 4.Document all ICWA inquiry and findings. Absence of documentation = absence of inquiry in litigation.
ICWA — Federal Law Supersedes State
ICWA is federal law. In applicable cases it supersedes state child welfare standards. Tribal notification is not optional. Non-compliance voids placement proceedings.
Source: ICWA 25 CFR Part 23 · Title IV-E 45 CFR ¥ 1356.21 · CAPTA 42 U.S.C. ¥ 5101
S-12Reentry — Justice-Involved Client Second Chance Act⌄
  1. 1.Confirm supervision status: probation, parole, or unsupervised release. This determines what the client can and cannot do.
  2. 2.SSI: if released within 12 months of suspension — bring release papers to SSA office, no new application required. Over 12 months — rew application. (42 U.S.C. ¥ 1382(e)(1))
  3. 3.Medicaid: suspended not terminated as of Jan 1, 2026. Reinstatement process, not reapplication. (Consolidated Appropriations Act 2024)
  4. 4.SNAP drug felony: check your state. Many states have lifted the ban.
  5. 5.Employment background check: Fair Chance Act prohibits asking about criminal history before conditional offer for federal contractors. (P.L. 116-92, effective Dec 2021)
Second Chance Act — 2024 Reauthorization
Second Chance Act reauthorized — passed Senate December 2024. Federal reentry funding and programming support remains active.
Source: Second Chance Act P.L. 110-199 · Fair Chance Act P.L. 116-92 · SSI 42 U.S.C. ¥ 1382(e)(1) · Consolidated Appropriations Act 2024
S-13Housing / Homelessness — HUD CoC Housing First⌄
  1. 1.Assess literal homelessness vs. at-risk: distinction affects which HUD CoC programs apply.
  2. 2.Housing First standard: sobriety, compliance, or treatment participation cannot be a precondition for housing access under CoC funding. (HUD CoC NOFO FY2024-2025)
  3. 3.For DV survivors: VAWA housing protections apply — emergency transfers, lease bifurcation, and prohibition on eviction based on DV-related criminal activity. (24 CFR Part 5 Subpart L)
  4. 4.SSI/SSDI SOAR model: if client appears to meet disability criteria, initiate SOAR-assisted application. (SAMHSA documented)
VAWA — Housing Protections
VAWA protects DV, sexual assault, dating violence, and stalking survivors in federally-assisted housing. Survivors cannot be evicted solely because of DV-related criminal activity by their abuser.
Source: HUD Housing First CoC NOFO FY2024-2025 · VAWA 24 CFR Part 5 Subpart L · SSI/SSDI SOAR (SAMHSA)
S-14Older Adult — OAA / APS Services OAA Title III⌄
  1. 1.Confirm age eligibility for OAA Title III programs: generally 60+ (60+ for most; 55+ for some programs).
  2. 2.Screen for: nutrition needs, caregiver stress, transportation, legal assistance, and health promotion eligibility under OAA.
  3. 3.If APS is already involved: coordinate, do not duplicate. Get a release and confirm the APS case worker's current assessment.
  4. 4.For mental health needs: MHPAEA Final Rule effective November 22, 2024 — mental health and SUD benefits must be covered at parity with medical/surgical benefits. (Sept 9, 2024 Final Rule)
  5. 5.Document: services offered, accepted, declined, and rationale in all cases.
MHPAEA — Parity Final Rule (Nov 22, 2024)
Mental health and SUD benefits must be covered at parity with medical/surgical benefits. If a client is being denied mental health coverage their medical plan would cover for a comparable condition — this is a MHPAEA violation.
Source: OAA Title III · Elder Justice Act APS Final Rule (June 7, 2024) · MHPAEA Final Rule (effective Nov 22, 2024)
