The Problem

Social workers are trained for the case.
Not for the moment inside it.

Organizations invest in training, supervision, and policy manuals. Workers complete them, pass their certifications, and go into the field.

Then the moments start. A client escalates faster than expected. A disclosure requires a mandatory report and the worker isn’t certain of the threshold. A benefit denial needs to be delivered with a redirect the worker can’t fully remember.

This is not a training failure. Training prepared the worker for the concept. It didn’t prepare them for this specific moment, with this specific person, right now.

Caseline℠ is built for that gap. Not a manual. Not an app. A guide that lives inside the moment — structured, specific, and ready the day the case is live.

What Caseline Covers

The situations your workers face every day.

Each Caseline guide is custom-built for your organization’s specific situations, protocols, and population. These are illustrative examples of what a Caseline guide can cover.

Illustrative situations — your Caseline is built from your source material
01
Client Crisis & De-escalation
Step-by-step de-escalation pathway for clients in acute distress. What to say, what not to say, when to involve a supervisor, when to call 911.
02
Mandatory Reporting Threshold
Reporting obligations surfaced in real time. Threshold criteria, required language, documentation steps, and who to notify first.
03
Benefit Denial & Redirect
What to say when a client doesn’t qualify. Alternative pathways, referral language, and how to close the conversation with a next step.
04
Intake with Resistant Client
Protocols for completing intake when a client is unwilling, guarded, or combative. What questions to prioritize, what to defer, how to proceed.
05
Unexpected Disclosure
When a client discloses something outside the scope of the current case. How to respond, what to document, whether to open a new case.
06
Safety Assessment
Live safety assessment framework for domestic situations, child welfare concerns, or imminent risk. Structured questions. Clear escalation thresholds.
