The Methodology

Six components. Every deliverable.

The same architecture underlies every Rudolph CX deliverable, enterprise or community-facing. We don't change the structure for different audiences — we change the language. The structure is what makes it work.

01
Layer Two Structural Context

Before the steps come the people the guidance is built for. Named explicitly. Returning citizens. Caregivers. First-generation anything. Naming the reader is the first act of respect.

02
Five-Operation Panel Architecture

Every situation resolves through six rendered elements: First Thing, Never Say, Remember, What YOU Do, What to SAY, and Escalation. Confidence-restoration close. No filler.

03
Room Classification

Every panel classified by emotional load — Default Room, Buffer Room, High-Stakes Room. Classification drives voice, pacing, and escalation discipline.

04
Dignity Ratio

A reader-side discipline. Every panel audited for whether it meets the reader where they are — or asks the reader to meet us where we are. The former passes. The latter is rewritten.

05
Paired-Team Production

Every deliverable runs through five paired roles before ship: Legal/Compliance, Marketing/Voice, Sales/Objection, Financial Strategy, Operations. Each pair has scope boundaries and handoff protocol.

06
Continuity Substrate

Every project carries a canonical document substrate — locks, protocols, identity, continuity index. Built for handoff. Built for audit. Built to outlast any single conversation.

The Process

From intake to delivery in three stages.

No surprises. No mystery. Here's what every engagement looks like.

01 · INTAKE
We collect everything before a line is written.

Job descriptions, SOPs, knowledge base exports, CRM workflows, onboarding materials. A 20-minute discovery call identifies the highest-frequency call types and the most common failure points. No build starts until we know what we're building for.

02 · BUILD
Content organized by situation, not category.

Every situation built with the full case setup, two-column steps, customer scripts, hard stops, and case-note templates. Six-component methodology applied throughout. Every deliverable runs through five-paired-team review before ship.

03 · DELIVERY
Working tool, day one.

Delivered as a working interactive guide your team uses on live calls. Not training videos. Not LMS modules. Not a deck. A tool. Includes documentation infrastructure built for handoff and audit.
