Process

How a guide gets built.

Done-for-you. You send the source material. We deliver a finished, deployable HTML guide. Your agents open it in any browser — no software required.

01 — Scope
Discovery Call

We research your operation before we get on the call. You describe your most common situations. We scope the guide. You receive a written proposal.

02 — Source
You Send the Material

SOPs, job aids, knowledge base articles, call recordings — whatever you have. We work with what exists. We do not need it to be organized.

03 — Build
We Structure It

Every situation gets case setup, a step sequence, an agent script, and a case note template — structured inside the Five-Operation Panel Architecture.

04 — Deliver
You Receive the Guide

A single HTML file. Opens in any browser. No internet required after delivery. Share it, print it, deploy it. Your guide, your branding.

Deliverable

What every guide includes.

Each situation is fully structured. No loose articles. No buried job aids. Everything an agent needs — in the moment the call is live.

  • Case Setup
    Case type, sub-type, origin, priority, and escalation path — pre-filled so agents open the case correctly the first time.
  • Step Sequence
    Numbered, ordered steps for every situation. Built around your actual procedures — not a generic script.
  • Agent Script
    Opening language, empathy language, and resolution language — written for your operation, your callers, your policies.
  • Case Note Template
    A structured note format agents can fill in before the call closes. Consistent documentation across every agent on every shift.
  • Escalation Path
    When to escalate, who to escalate to, and what information to have ready — built into each situation.
  • Quick Reference Index
    Standard tier and above include a searchable index across all situations. Agents find what they need in seconds.

The Work Behind the Work

This was built from inside
the problem.

Aidline was not designed in a conference room. It was built by someone who sat in the agent seat — handling federal employee FSA claims, navigating Ohio unemployment systems, and working inside contact center environments where the calls came in faster than the training covered them.

The gap was always the same: multiple systems, constantly shifting policies, high-stakes callers, and no single reference that met the agent where the call was actually happening.

Rudolph CX exists because that guide should have existed — and now it does. Built to your operation. Structured to your situations. Delivered as a finished tool your agents can open the moment the call comes in.
