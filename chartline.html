The Problem

Records coordinators are trained for the process.
Not for the moment inside it.

Healthcare organizations invest in HIPAA training, compliance certifications, and policy documentation. Coordinators complete them, understand the rules, and go to the phones.

Then the live moments start. An attorney demands records under a court deadline and the authorization on file is expired. A caller fails HIPAA verification and escalates. Records go out to the wrong patient. An unauthorized requester pushes back hard.

This is not a training failure. Training prepared the coordinator for HIPAA. It didn’t prepare them for this specific attorney, this specific authorization failure, right now.

Chartline is built for that gap. Not a policy manual. Not another system login. A guide that lives inside the moment — HIPAA-compliant, role-specific, and ready the day the coordinator goes live.

What Chartline Covers

The moments that define records compliance.

Each situation is built from HIPAA requirements and verified against HHS enforcement patterns. Your Chartline is then built from your organization’s actual policies, authorization standards, and platform workflows.

Illustrative situations — your Chartline is built from your source material
01
Request Status Inquiry
Attorney or adjuster checking request status. Standard status check path, court deadline variant, and the exact language for delays. No PHI without verification.
⚠ HIPAA · Verify before status disclosure
02
New Records Request
Opening a new request and gathering required information. Standard intake and rush/urgent variant. Authorization collection, case creation, caller type identification.
⚠ HIPAA · Authorization required before intake
03
HIPAA Verification Failure
Caller cannot verify identity to access PHI. Cannot-verify path and partial-verification path. Hard stop on PHI release without completed verification.
⚠ PHI · No access without completed verification
04
Rush / Court Deadline
Urgent request with legal deadline. Court deadline escalation path and general rush path. Authorization must still be valid — urgency does not override HIPAA.
⚠ HIPAA · Deadline does not waive authorization requirement
05
Records Incomplete or Wrong
Request fulfilled but records missing pages or belong to wrong patient. Missing records path and wrong patient path. Immediate escalation on wrong-patient events.
⚠ PHI Breach Risk · Wrong patient = immediate escalation
06
Unauthorized Requester
Caller requesting records without valid authorization. No-authorization path and expired-authorization path. No PHI disclosed under any pressure from caller.
⚠ HIPAA Hard Stop · No authorization = no records
07
Invoice & Billing Question
Billing disputes, invoice copies, and payment questions. Invoice copy path and billing dispute path. Billing information does not require same PHI verification thresholds.
08
Medical Canvassing Request
Request to locate and identify treating providers for a patient during a specified date range. New canvass intake path and canvass status check. Chain of custody documentation required.
⚠ HIPAA · Authorization scope must cover canvassing
